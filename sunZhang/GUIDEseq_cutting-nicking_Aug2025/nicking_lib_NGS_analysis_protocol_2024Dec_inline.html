3. Keep the reverse randomized EIF3D target sequence below

NNCCNNNNNNNNNNNNNNNTCGTCTNN, 20bp in the center

Among 20nt on-target, 14 bases are randomized for each library. This is an example of Lib1.

From lib1 to lib7, move forward one base in the target and do the 14 randomized again, until the last 20th base. Keep the rest of the structure outside of 20nt spacer unchanged.

Ns represent randomized bases (A/T/C/G)

Lib1: NNNNNNNNNNNNNNTCGTCT

Lib2: TNNNNNNNNNNNNNNCGTCT

Lib3: TGNNNNNNNNNNNNNNGTCT

Lib4: TGCNNNNNNNNNNNNNNTCT

Lib5: TGCGNNNNNNNNNNNNNNCT

Lib6: TGCGGNNNNNNNNNNNNNNT

Lib7: TGCGGANNNNNNNNNNNNNN

4. Flip the reverse sequence to the EIF3D on-target sequence with PAM:

5'- AGACGACCCTGTCATCCGCANGG-3' (change the direction of sequence)

5. the sequence different from the on-target sequence: 5'-AGACGACCCTGTCATCCGCANGG-3' is identified as off-target nicking, and the sequence consistent with 5'- AGACGACCCTGTCATCCGCANGG-3' is identified as on-target nicking; on-target nicking is always higher than off-target editing.

6. identify the sequence tolerance by the number of mismatches (MM)

E.g. 5'-TAGGAATCAAACACTTTTATTGG-3' on-target

MM1 could be any one mismatch on any 14nt position, like TAGGAAgCAAACACTTTTATTGG

MM2 could be any two mismatches within any 14nt position, like TAGGAAgtAAACACTTTTATTGG

7. Count the 14nt randomized sequence combinations (on-target and off-target total number)

So we finally get the distribution of off-target based on mismatches numbers, as well as the off-target total counts for each treated group.
